My Personal Skincare Mistakes and Lessons Learned

Over the years, I have struggled with acne and made countless mistakes that worsened my skin. Below are some of the most important lessons I have learned from my experience, and what you can do instead to avoid the same mistakes.

Image source: Pexels

1. Trying a New Acne Treatment Every Week

My Experience: I used to hop between products weekly, hoping to see instant results. Instead, I ended up irritating my skin further, and it became red and flaky.

Why it was Bad: Acne treatments take time to work. Switching frequently does not allow your skin to adjust and heal.

What to Do Instead: Stick to one treatment for at least 6 to 8 weeks before deciding whether it works. Remember, be patient!

Image source: Pexels

2. Using Dirty Makeup Brushes

My Experience: I rarely cleaned my makeup brushes, not realizing they were full of bacteria. I started noticing breakouts in the same areas where I applied makeup.

Why it was Bad: Dirty brushes transfer bacteria, oil, and old makeup onto your skin, clogging your pores.

What to Do Instead: Clean your brushes weekly with a gentle cleanser and let them air dry completely.

Image source: Pexels

3. Using Soap and Hair Care Products That Clog Pores

My Experience: I did not realize that my shampoo and conditioner were full of oils that dripped onto my face, causing breakouts around my hairline.

Why it was Bad: Hair products with oils or silicones can clog your pores if they come into contact with your skin.

What to Do Instead: Look for non-comedogenic hair care products and rinse thoroughly to avoid residue.

Image source: Pexels

4. Sleeping in My Makeup

My Experience: After a long day, I used to go to bed without removing my makeup. I woke up with clogged pores and irritated skin.

Why it was Bad: Makeup traps dirt and oil, causing breakouts if left on overnight.

What to Do Instead: Always remove your makeup before bed using a gentle cleanser or micellar water.

Image source: Pexels

5. Scrubbing My Skin Harshly

My Experience: I thought scrubbing harder would clean my skin better. Instead, I ended up with red, irritated patches.

Why it was Bad: Scrubbing strips your skin of its natural oils, leading to irritation and increased oil production.

What to Do Instead: Use a gentle exfoliator once or twice a week and always be gentle with your skin.

Image source: Pexels

6. Popping Pimples

My Experience: I could not resist popping my pimples, which led to scars and more inflamed acne.

Why it was Bad: Popping pimples pushes bacteria deeper into your skin, causing infections and scarring.

What to Do Instead: Apply a spot treatment with salicylic acid or benzoyl peroxide and let it heal naturally.
